Chennai Central

Chennai Central, erstwhile Madras Central, is the main railway terminus in the city of Chennai, formerly known as Madras. It lies adjacent to the current headquarters of the Southern Railway, as well as the Ripon Building, and is one of the most important railway hubs in South India.

Madras Central was built in 1873 at Parktown as a second terminus to decongest the Royapuram harbour station, which was being utilised for port movements.

The clock tower with the flagstaff, the tallest of the towers of the main building, has four faces and reaches a height of 136 ft.[27] It is set to chime every quarter of an hour and every hour.
